# Executive CV Skill — UK C-Suite & Senior Leadership

## Overview

This skill creates professional, ATS-friendly executive CVs following UK best practice for CTO, CEO, COO, CFO, CIO, Director, NED, and equivalent senior leadership roles. It produces a .docx file using the docx skill.

**Always read the docx skill first** (`/mnt/skills/public/docx/SKILL.md`) before generating the document.

## When to Use

- User requests a CV for any C-suite, VP, SVP, MD, Director, or board-level role
- User wants to upgrade an existing CV for executive-level job search
- User is preparing for engagement with executive search firms / headhunters
- User mentions roles with P&L ownership, board reporting, or multi-site/global scope

## Executive CV Principles (UK)

### Format Rules
- **Length:** 2 pages (3 only if scope is genuinely complex and evidence-led)
- **Layout:** Single-column, reverse chronological, ATS-safe
- **Paper:** A4 (11906 x 16838 DXA)
- **Font:** Calibri or similar clean sans-serif, 10–11pt body
- **No photo** — UK best practice
- **No date of birth, marital status, nationality, or full address** — just City, UK
- **File format:** PDF recommended for submission; .docx for editing
- **Date format:** Month Year (e.g. January 2020 – Present)

### Required Sections (in order)
1. **Header** — Full name (large, prominent), target role tagline, contact line (city, phone, email, LinkedIn URL)
2. **Executive Profile** — 3–5 lines. Not generic. Must include: years of experience, scale/scope managed, 1–2 headline achievements with metrics, leadership positioning statement. Written in third person or neutral tone (no "I").
3. **Core Competencies** — Grouped into 3 keyword clusters (e.g. Strategic & Commercial / Technology & Delivery / Leadership & People). These must be tailored to the target role. 4–5 terms per cluster.
4. **Career History** — Reverse chronological. For each role include:
   - Job title, company name, location, dates (in a clean layout)
   - Scope line (italic): budget, headcount, reporting line, geographic reach
   - 3–5 achievement bullets (most recent roles) or 1–2 lines (older roles)
   - Every bullet must follow the pattern: **Action → Scope → Quantified Outcome**
5. **Selected Achievements** (optional) — A highlight box with 4 headline metrics (£Xm, X%, team growth, etc.) for visual impact
6. **Education & Qualifications** — Degree, institution, year. No grades unless exceptional and recent.
7. **Professional Development & Certifications** — Relevant certs (TOGAF, AWS, PRINCE2, etc.), executive programmes
8. **Board & Advisory Roles** (if applicable) — NED, advisory board, mentoring
9. **Professional Memberships** (if applicable) — BCS, IoD, CEng, etc.
10. **References available on request** — centred, italic, at bottom

### Optional: EVR Narrative Framework

Adapted from the E-V-R Congruence model (Thompson & Martin, 2010) — originally a strategic management framework for evaluating leadership fit. This can optionally be used to structure the Executive Profile section or to frame career history narratives at a higher strategic level. Mark as optional — the standard approach (achievement bullets with STAR/CAR) is the default.

**E — Environment:** The market, sector, and business context you operated in. Shows situational awareness and the complexity of the landscape.
- Example: "Joined during a period of rapid market consolidation, with the business facing margin erosion from three new market entrants and a legacy technology estate blocking product innovation."

**V — Vision:** The strategic direction you set, your leadership philosophy, and what you drove the organisation towards. Shows strategic thinking and intent.
- Example: "Defined and championed a platform-first strategy, repositioning the company from bespoke project delivery to a scalable SaaS model aligned to emerging customer demand."

**R — Resources:** What you managed and deployed to deliver — budget, people, technology, partnerships, governance structures. Shows scope and execution capability.
- Example: "Mobilised a £12m transformation budget, built a cross-functional delivery team of 85, and established a governance framework with quarterly board reporting and investor oversight."

**When to use EVR:**
- In the Executive Profile to frame your overall career narrative
- As an optional intro paragraph before achievement bullets in a major role
- When the candidate has a strong turnaround, transformation, or market-entry story

**When NOT to use EVR:**
- As a replacement for quantified achievement bullets (always keep those)
- For every role — use it for the 1–2 most significant positions only
- When the candidate prefers a concise, metrics-only approach

The EVR narrative sits *above* the achievement bullets, providing strategic context. The bullets then provide the evidence.

### Achievement Bullet Formula

Every career bullet must demonstrate executive-level impact:

**Bad:** "Responsible for technology strategy"
**Good:** "Defined and delivered 3-year technology roadmap, migrating 12 legacy systems to cloud-native architecture and reducing operational costs by £1.4m p.a."

**Bad:** "Managed a large team"  
**Good:** "Built and scaled engineering function from 30 to 150 across UK, India, and Poland, establishing career frameworks that reduced attrition from 25% to 8%"

**Pattern:** [Strong verb] + [what you did] + [scale/scope] + [quantified outcome with £/% metric]

### Executive Signal Checklist

The CV must clearly demonstrate:
- [ ] **Commercial impact** — revenue growth, cost savings, margin improvement, with £ figures
- [ ] **Scale** — budget size, team size, geographic reach, number of sites/products
- [ ] **Governance** — board reporting, investor relations, regulatory, audit
- [ ] **Stakeholder complexity** — C-suite peers, external partners, PE/VC, unions
- [ ] **Strategic delivery** — multi-year programmes, transformation, M&A integration
- [ ] **Leadership systems** — frameworks, cadences, capability building, succession

### Role-Specific Guidance

**CTO / CIO:**
- Emphasise: technology strategy, architecture decisions, engineering culture, innovation, security posture, vendor management, build-vs-buy decisions
- Keywords: enterprise architecture, cloud migration, platform engineering, data strategy, AI/ML, DevOps, SRE, technology governance, TOGAF, ISO 27001

**CEO / MD:**
- Emphasise: vision-setting, P&L ownership, investor/board relations, growth strategy, M&A, culture
- Keywords: revenue growth, market expansion, fundraising, IPO readiness, business model, stakeholder management, corporate governance

**COO:**
- Emphasise: operational efficiency, process improvement, multi-site management, supply chain, service delivery, scaling
- Keywords: operational excellence, Lean/Six Sigma, OTIF, cost-to-serve, SLA performance, change management, ERP, shared services

**CFO:**
- Emphasise: financial strategy, reporting, fundraising, compliance, risk management
- Keywords: FP&A, cash flow, audit, treasury, IFRS, tax planning, investor relations, due diligence

**Executive Head Teacher / MAT CEO:**
- Emphasise: school improvement, multi-site leadership, Ofsted outcomes, budget management, safeguarding, community engagement, curriculum strategy
- Keywords: multi-academy trust, pupil outcomes, Progress 8, curriculum design, DfE, governance, safeguarding, SEND, staff development, parental engagement, school improvement plan, trust-wide strategy

### Keyword Strategy

- Group keywords into the Core Competencies clusters
- Repeat critical keywords in achievement bullets (not just the skills section)
- Use business language, not internal jargon: "margin" not "numbers", "delivery performance" not "getting stuff done"
- Ensure LinkedIn headline and About section mirror the CV positioning

## Workflow

1. **Gather information** — Ask the user for: target role(s), career history with dates, key achievements with metrics, education, certifications, and any specific requirements
2. **Read the docx skill** — Always read `/mnt/skills/public/docx/SKILL.md` before creating the document
3. **Generate the CV** — Create a professionally formatted .docx using the template structure above
4. **Validate** — Run the docx validation script
5. **Present** — Save to outputs and present to the user

## Design Specification

- Colour palette: Navy headers (#1B365D), slate body text (#4A5568), light blue-grey backgrounds (#F0F4F8) for competency/achievement boxes
- Section headings: uppercase, letter-spaced, navy, with a divider line below
- Role blocks: title in navy bold, company in slate, dates right-aligned
- Achievement bullets: small square bullet (▪), indented
- Competency grid: 3-column table with light background, no borders
- Achievement highlights: 2×2 grid with large navy numbers

## Information Gathering Questions

If the user hasn't provided their details, ask these (adapt based on what's already known):

1. What role(s) are you targeting? (e.g. CTO, CEO, COO — or a mix?)
2. Walk me through your career history — for each role I need: job title, company, location, dates, and 3–5 headline achievements with numbers
3. What's your biggest commercial achievement? (£ revenue, cost savings, growth %)
4. What scale have you operated at? (budget, team size, locations, reporting line)
5. Education — degrees and institutions?
6. Certifications or executive programmes?
7. Any board, NED, or advisory roles?
8. Professional memberships?
9. Anything specific you want to emphasise or any roles you're targeting at specific companies?
